Having been convicted of Synergism under the Court of Saxe-Weimar, Professor Beautiful was renditioned to a small jail cell in Texas, whence he encountered a man in finery, wig and hose. He introduced himself as Mister Bach Jangles. Humming a few ditties in four parts, he explained "I drinks a bit." Inspired, the Professor broke off a drain pipe, twisted in a pecuilar way that brought to Mr. Bach's eyes a look of wonder and not a little fear. "Relax, old man, the tuba hasn't even been invented yet. Until now." The old man was heard to remark under his vaporous breath, "son, that ain't no cello".


Years later, PB was publicly accused of creating dangerous music before a crowd of a thousand college students. This time, no jail sentence was imposed. Instead, he was conferred the honor of an empty envelope. Stanley Clarke got one too.
